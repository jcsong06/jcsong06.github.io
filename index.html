I'm a computer science PhD candidate at Stanford University studying artificial intelligence. I completed a master's in CS from Stanford and received the Christopher Stephenson Memorial Award for the best CS master's thesis. My current research is on back-and-forth validation of critical autonomy systems, focusing on reinforcement learning, Bayesian optimization, and robust methods for controlling uncertain systems.

I'm currently a grad research assistant at the Stanford Intelligent Systems Laboratory (SISL) advised by Mykel Kochenderfer and a part of the Stanford Center for AI Safety and the Stanford School of Sustainability.

Previously, I was a research staff member at MIT Lincoln Laboratory and was part of the team that developed, optimized, and validated the next-generation aircraft collision avoidance systems, certified by the FAA.

And I love Julia.
